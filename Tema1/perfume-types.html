Perfume Types

Explore the fascinating world of fragrances and discover various perfume families

Understanding Fragrance Types

Discover how perfumes are categorized and what makes each type unique

Perfumes are classified into different categories based on their concentration of aromatic compounds. The higher the concentration, the longer the fragrance will last on your skin.

Parfum

20-30% concentration

Lasts 6-8 hours

Eau de Parfum

15-20% concentration

Lasts 4-5 hours

Eau de Toilette

5-15% concentration

Lasts 2-3 hours

Eau de Cologne

2-4% concentration

Lasts 2 hours

Women's Fragrance Families

The most popular fragrance families for women

Floral

The most popular family for women, floral fragrances are built around the scent of flowers such as rose, jasmine, gardenia, and lily of the valley.

Notable Examples:

  • Chanel No. 5
  • Miss Dior
  • Gucci Bloom

Oriental

Rich and exotic fragrances that feature warm, sweet notes like vanilla, amber, musk, and spices. These perfumes are sensual and often worn for evening occasions.

Notable Examples:

  • Yves Saint Laurent Black Opium
  • Lancome La Vie Est Belle
  • Tom Ford Black Orchid

Fresh

Clean, light fragrances that include citrus, aquatic and green notes. These perfumes are perfect for daytime wear, especially in spring and summer.

Notable Examples:

  • Dolce & Gabbana Light Blue
  • Issey Miyake L'Eau d'Issey
  • Marc Jacobs Daisy

Men's Fragrance Families

The most popular fragrance families for men

Woody

Masculine fragrances built around warm, dry wood notes such as sandalwood, cedar, and vetiver. Often combined with aromatic and citrus notes.

Notable Examples:

  • Tom Ford Oud Wood
  • Dior Sauvage
  • Chanel Bleu de Chanel

Aromatic

These fragrances feature herbal notes like lavender, rosemary, and thyme, often combined with citrus and spicy elements. Fresh and masculine.

Notable Examples:

  • Giorgio Armani Acqua di Gio
  • Dior Homme
  • Paco Rabanne 1 Million

Spicy

Bold fragrances featuring notes like pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. Often combined with woody or oriental bases for depth.

Notable Examples:

  • Jean Paul Gaultier Le Male
  • Versace Eros
  • Givenchy Gentleman

How to Choose a Perfume

Tips for selecting the perfect fragrance for you

1

Know Your Preferences

Take note of scents you naturally gravitate towards in everyday products like candles, soaps, or lotions.

2

Test Before Buying

Always test fragrances on your skin, not just on paper strips. Perfumes react differently with your body chemistry.

3

Give It Time

Wait a few hours to experience how the fragrance develops. Top notes fade quickly, while base notes last longer.

4

Consider the Occasion

Choose lighter fragrances for day and work, and save richer, more intense scents for evening and special occasions.
